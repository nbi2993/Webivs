LEGNAXE In The Universe

Character List

The LEGNAXE universe is a vast and complex world where ancient races, advanced technology, and mystical magic coexist. This is the backdrop for great wars, shocking discoveries, and the intertwined destinies of souls.

THE CREATOR

The Creator

Description: The supreme entity who created all things, including Heaven, Earth, angels, and humans. Has no specific form but is perceived through boundless presence, infinite light, and a voice echoing everywhere. Their will is absolute but sometimes incomprehensible to angels. The Creator demonstrates love, judgment, and ultimately, withdrawal to grant self-determination to creations.

I. ARCHANGELS

Lucifer: The Fallen Angel, Once the Morning Star

Appearance: Sharp features, luminous eyes (shifting from deep blue to fiery red when enraged), pale skin almost glowing with silver veins. Long, lustrous black hair. Once magnificent wings with silvery-white feathers tinged with gold, now dull, tattered, and faintly luminous. Tall, well-proportioned physique, exuding nobility yet full of arrogance.

Weapon: A brilliant sword of light, once forged from heavenly fire, now stained black, emitting destructive energy.

Personality: Charismatic, ambitious, arrogant, intelligent, charming, yet torn by betrayal and hatred. Capable of persuading anyone, with a heart yearning for absolute power.

Imagery: A noble face, high nose bridge, thin lips, sharp eyes like a great orator.

Michael: Leader of the Heavenly Host

Appearance: Square, strong face, deep brown eyes that are calm and determined. Healthy bronze skin, glowing as if bathed in sunlight. Short, neat dark brown hair. Large wings with pure white feathers edged in gold, emitting a radiant light. Muscular body, standing majestically like a warrior.

Weapon: A blazing sword of fire, a symbol of justice and divine power.

Personality: Brave, loyal, strict, always prioritizing duty over personal emotions, yet possessing deep compassion.

Imagery: A resolute face, square jaw, steadfast eyes, evoking the image of a commanding general.

Gabriel: The Messenger Angel

Imagery: A graceful face, arched eyebrows, full lips, like a poet with timeless beauty.

Personality: Benevolent, compassionate, patient, always listening and healing both body and soul with gentleness yet immense power.

Appearance: Sharp face, keen silver-gray eyes that seem to see through the soul. Olive-toned skin, shimmering as if illuminated by light. Short, jet-black hair, naturally tousled. Amenadiel: The Respected Angel

(Appears from Part 3)

IV. HUMAN CHARACTERS AND OTHER DIVINE BEINGS

Adam

Description: The first man created on Earth. Tall, robust, and strong. Terracotta-colored skin, tanned, faintly glowing. Slightly wavy black hair, deep innocent brown eyes. Moves gracefully, in harmony with the rhythm of the Earth. Initially pure, accepting everything. After eating the forbidden fruit, becomes aware of nakedness and shame, facing hardship and despair.

Lilith

Description: The first woman created on Earth, at the same time as Adam. Sharp, authoritative beauty. Long black hair like a silk waterfall. Eyes burning like embers, filled with curiosity and a wild, untamed fire. Smaller than Adam but exuding confidence. Her movements and gaze reveal a mind yearning to understand and question. Unwilling to be confined under Adam's shadow, she left Eden. Returns after a harsh exile, carrying weariness and deep sorrow.

Eve

Description: The second woman created, from Adam's rib. A fragile yet strong physique, with features imbued with both earthly and divine beauty. Lustrous hair like golden threads, skin faintly glowing under moonlight, eyes containing the warmth of a thousand dawns. Gentler, softer, more pliable than Lilith. After eating the forbidden fruit, her mind ignites with wisdom and understanding, but also carries discontent and rebellion.

Cain

Description: Eve and Adam's firstborn son, born after their exile. A child of strength and passion. Grew up as a farmer. Has dark eyes concealing unspoken thoughts. Envious of Abel. Becomes the first murderer in human history after killing Abel and becomes a fugitive, bearing a mark of protection yet also a curse. Later built the city of Enoch and became a leader for his people, carrying the legacy and lessons from his sin.

Abel

Description: Lilith's son, born under the silver moon. Carries the serenity his parents lost. A shepherd, favored by Adam over Cain, praised for his "harmony" with nature.

Noah

Description: The mortal chosen by the Creator to survive the devastating Great Flood, carrying the sole hope for humanity's rebirth. Prophesied by Gabriel about the coming flood and given detailed instructions on how to build the great Ark. Noah obeyed, gathering his family and animals as instructed, steadfast in his faith despite ridicule from those around him. After the flood receded and he stepped onto dry land, he witnessed the Creator's rainbow promise, a covenant indicating that life would continue.

Hidden Depth: Although called "righteous" in this event, a big question arises: Was Noah truly pure, or did he also harbor inherent human sins and selfishness? Was his selection merely a random decision, a coincidence of fate, or simply being the "least bad" in a world that had become too corrupt? Or perhaps, the Great Flood was not entirely a moral punishment, but a cold "Reset button" pressed by the Creator in the early stages of creation, when the concept of "love" was still alien or undefined, a way to erase mistakes and start anew? Noah's existence and actions, therefore, carry a more complex layer of meaning, raising questions about the nature of righteousness and the Creator's ultimate purpose at the dawn of the world.

Mary

Description: The mother who shelters the child bearing light from the Most High. Holding the child in her arms, she witnesses Lucifer's attack and the angels' protection. Believes in the light her son carries.

Joseph

Description: The protector of Mary and the child in Bethlehem. Stands guard, defending his family from danger. Determined to face those who seek to harm the child.
